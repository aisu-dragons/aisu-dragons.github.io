introduction —

This project was researched, prototyped, designed, and tested as part of professor Clark Delashmet’s IACT 315: human/computer interaction course at SCAD. all work was done over 10 weeks period in Fall 2020.

problem

As technology becomes more involved with our everyday lives, our capabilities as humans expand to achieve things. The problems that arise out of unchecked technological innovations are the following:

Privacy
Farming of personal information to send to physical junk mail
COVID-19 Pandemic
Virtual shopping accessibility for small business during COVID-19
Mental Health
Technology's negative impact effect on health

Children have excessive usage of screens as parents make it an easy solution to buy time for themselves.


From the three initial topics, we proceed with technology’s negative effect on health and specifically target workers who work on average of 40 hours per week. Our target group started out as office workers where we try to inform and promote healthy screen usage. We noticed that this target group already has a highly saturated market, so we transitioned our focus to a younger generation in hopes to prevent these issues or slow it down with the rise of technology.

primary research —

As technology becomes more involved with our everyday lives, our capabilities as humans expand to achieve things. The problems that arise out of unchecked technological innovations are privacy, health, crime, and mental well being.

Children have excessive usage of screens as parents make it an easy solution to buy time for themselves.

When and why are they using technology?
From the survery responeses, 33% of parents stated that they use technology as a tool to distract their child.
How much time does your child spend on screen?
From the survey responeses, 21% of parents stated that their children spend more than 2 hours looking at the screen. The average american kid of the age group of 0-5 is spending more time recommended (2 hours or less).

With these quantitative data, we were happy to realize that most of our users fell under the category of “less than an hour.” There was still a chunk of people that were spending more than the recommended amount of time looking at screens.

I was involved with the user interview processes and conducting interviews. We decided our main goal from the user interview is to get a better understanding of how children are learning and playing and how the parents’ viewpoint on-screen usage.

Through our interviews and survey responses, we realized that…

  1. Parents give devices to kids in order to keep them busy and give themselves time (ie. cooking and driving)

  2. Children are still able to enjoy playing with toys and going outside

prototyping & user testing —

Having decided on a baloo, we created an interactive pdf of a storyline to test the concept, complexity, and usability. we wrote a short storyline flowchart that would involve the child by having them give inputs into the story at decision points.

I was involved with the user testing and tested components of the interactive pdf. We received feedback from parents about the child’s attention span on the stories and concerns about how complex the stories are. ​

low-fidelity wireframes —

We all created low-fidelity wireframes and talked about what we liked about each of them before creating the final high fidelity wireframes. I created the basic lofi wireframes with an idea of keeping it simple and not too much customization for busy parents.
